Chapter 9 The Trickster

 

  1  Geller at SRI: The account of Geller’s first visit to SRI comes mainly from interviews with Puthoff, supplemented by Puthoff and Targ 1977, Wilhelm 1976, and interviews with Geller.

  2  Geller’s life: Geller; Geller and Playfair 1986; Gardner 1981; Randi 1982.

  3  Dayan: Geller and Playfair 1986, pp. 194–95.

  4  Yariv denial: Wilhelm 1976, p. 177.

  5  Elisha: 2 Kings 6:8–12.

  6  CIA interested: Puthoff admitted that his contract “sponsor” was interested in Geller. A former senior CIA official also noted the agency’s interest in Geller.

  7  wild surmises: Puthoff.

  8  Geller’s secret meetings: Geller.

  9  Israelis visit: Puthoff.

10  Puthoff paranoid: Puthoff (“more paranoid” is a direct quote). Geller says that the Israeli intelligence men were genuinely interested in hearing SRI’s opinions since they hadn’t been able to keep him in Israel to perform their own experiments.

11  some visits not reported: Geller recalls being asked by Puthoff and Targ, on several occasions, not to sign in as an official visitor at SRI. (A similar policy was used for the Fort Meade remote viewers, who were asked to sign in under the name Scotty Watt.)

12  Geller’s feats at SRI: Puthoff, Wilhelm 1976, and Puthoff and Targ 1977.

13  “Geller caught” rumor: Swann; Puthoff.

14  SRI suspected fraud: Puthoff.

15  mythology of shamanism: Campbell 1984, p. 168.

16  Najagneq, for example: Ibid., p. 169.

17  mystifications, theatrical scenes: Ibid., p. 171.

18  trickster themes: For example, Ellenberger 1970, Ross 1989, Schnabel 1994.

19  Jung’s trickster: Jung 1959.

20  heat from skeptics: Puthoff.

21  Advanced Research Projects: Puthoff; Wilhelm 1976; Van de Castle.

22  no CIA money for Geller: Puthoff.

23  Kennett: A former researcher associated with the program and a former senior CIA official.

24  Mexico, New York, etc.: Geller and Playfair 1986; Geller. I saw Geller’s house during a visit in December 1994.
