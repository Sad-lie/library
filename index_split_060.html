Chapter 17 Blue

 

  1  high-priority target: Two sources formerly associated with the RV program, plus Graff 1995. Harary, citing security obligations, refused to discuss this with me when I first interviewed him in July 1994, but he later commented on it publicly during an appearance on Larry King Live on November 29, 1995. Lyons and Truzzi 1991, who briefly refer to the episode, report that there was some skepticism at the NSC over the usefulness of Harary’s data.

  2  Harary’s background: Harary; Puthoff.

  3  Harary versus Swann: Harary; Puthoff. Swann made it clear in interviews that he disliked Harary and often claimed that he had “had Keith fired from the program.”

  4  wanted controlled studies: A not very well-controlled study in this area was reported in Puthoff 1984a, pp. 89–90.

  5  experiments without coordinates: Puthoff; Harary; Puthoff and Targ 1975, 1977, 1979.

  6  “Why don’t you just say ‘target’?”: Puthoff; Harary.

  7  infighting at SRI: Harary; Swann.

  8  wanted their own project: Harary; Swann; Puthoff.

  9  Langford still RVed: A source formerly associated with the RV program.

10  Targ’s demise: Harary, Swann, and another source formerly associated with the RV program. A copy of the memo was provided to me (but not by Puthoff).

11  Targ’s high moral reasons: See, for example, Targ’s remarks in Anderson 1984.

12  Soviet tour: Harary; Targ and Harary 1984; Anderson 1984.

13  concerns at SRI and DIA: Two sources formerly associated with the RV program.

14  Targ’s work in Moscow: Anderson 1984. Although Targ’s work may have been innocuous, Colonel John Alexander later told me that the Institute of Theoretical Problems was deeply involved in military-oriented research.

15  Delphi Associates: Harary; Harary 1992.

16  lawsuit, angry correspondence: Harary 1992; Targ 1992.
