Chapter 6 Puthoff

 

  1  Puthoff’s life: Puthoff.

  2  $70 million in government contracts: Wilhelm 1976; Jaroff 1973a.

  3  magnetometer incident: Puthoff; Puthoff and Targ 1977. Swann provided a more dramatic account, which involved graduate students fleeing the lab in distress. In an interview in 1994 Arthur Hebard, builder of the magnetometer, disputed that Swann had done anything paranormal in his laboratory and blamed the disturbance in the output (which he says he later reproduced) on a glitch in a helium coolant line. Hebard’s account is contained in Randi 1982.

  4  Elisha: 2 Kings 6:8–12.

  5  oracles: Targ and Harary 1984.

  6  John Dee: Deacon 1968; McRae 1984.

  7  Czechs, Russians, Germans: Ebon 1983; Lyons and Truzzi 1991.

  8  Nautilus story: A lengthy account of the Science et Vie affair is given in Chapter 3 of Ebon 1983. It is also referred to by Ostrander and Schroeder 1970.

  9  Khrushchev: Soviet émigré Nikolai Kokhlov, allegedly a former KGB officer, in Starr and McQuaid 1985.

10  effect of Nautilus story: The sudden growth of Soviet parapsychology in the 1960s is discussed in Ostrander and Schroeder 1970, Lamothe 1972, and Ebon 1983.

11  Vasiliev quote: Groller 1986.

12  Kogan: Puthoff, Puthoff and Targ 1976.

13  Milan Ryzl: Puthoff; Puthoff 1984a, p. 92.

14  Naumov claims: Ostrander and Schroeder 1970.

15  DIA report: Lamothe 1972.

16  CIA skepticism: A former senior CIA official.

17  Kirlian photography: Ostrander and Schroeder 1970; Ebon 1973.

18  Kogan seemed more serious: Puthoff. Thompson also referred in interviews to the fact that much of Soviet psi research suddenly “went classified” in the late 1960s.

19  Nixon tale: Gottlieb’s testimony was on September 21, 1977, before Senator Edward Kennedy’s Subcommittee on Health, reported in Ebon 1983, pp. 116–17. Gottlieb denied Nixon’s involvement when asked directly by Kennedy but elsewhere said, “[T]he president when he came back described some unusual feelings he and others had and asked if I would be able to give counsel.” A former senior CIA official told me that he rejected the White House’s request to look into the matter, arguing that it was inappropriate for the CIA to be involved in White House medical issues. According to the official, he suggested that the White House contact a domestically oriented medical authority such as the U.S. surgeon general.

20  more monitoring of psi: A former senior CIA official; Puthoff.

21  Puthoff approached: Puthoff, a former senior CIA official.

22  October ’72: Puthoff; Swann; Puthoff and Targ 1977. I first confirmed the CIA connection from former senior CIA and Pentagon officials. Following the broadcast of Schnabel and Eagles 1995, the CIA officially admitted its involvement in a press release (September 6, 1995).
