1.18. Form

 

There are various ways of notating the form of a piece of music. These include using introductions and endings, as well as form letters or section titles.

 

Introductions

 

An introduction occurs before the body of the piece and is used to preface the piece itself.

 

Form Letters/Section Titles

 

Form letters and section title are used to cue the shifts between sections of a piece of music. In common Western music section titles often include: Intro, Verse, Pre-Chorus, Chorus, Bridge, Solo, Ending, etc.

 

Tags

 

A tag is an ending that extends the final phrase of a piece, often adding a few beats (sometimes less than a full bar in length).
