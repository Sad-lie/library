Chapter 19 Obi Swann

 

  1  Ed Dames: Dames; personal observation.

  2  out-of-body experience: Christine Dames.

  3  Dames interested in RV training: Dames; Swann; another source.

  4  Dames’s initiation and training: Dames. Other trainees who went through the same experience, plus Swann and Atwater and another source connected to the program, provided additional information and insights.

  5  Atwater’s mother: Atwater.

  6  Bob Monroe story: Monroe 1971; Atwater; official Monroe Institute literature; Druckman and Swets 1988.

  7  Atwater’s first visit: Atwater.

  8  sharing Hemi-Sync tapes: A former senior Army official; INSCOM 1984a, b.

  9  Buchanan’s OBE: Buchanan. Dames and others remember him talking about it.

10  outrage, brainwashing, hysteria: Dames; Atwater; Buchanan; another source who took the course.

11  life would never be the same: Dames.

12  Hemi-Sync experience: The descriptions of the tone states comes partly from the Monroe literature but mainly from my own impressions. I took the course in 1995, listening to the same tapes that Dames, Smith, and the others listened to.

13  dark circles: These are evident on a 1984 lecture Monroe videotaped, which I saw in 1995. One of Monroe’s daughters-in-law, who was a trainer at the course, described his sleep patterns for me.

14  McMoneagle research: McMoneagle 1993; Dames; and two other sources from the program.

15  Jesus, Miranon: I read the transcript of the Jesus session and heard the Miranon story when I was at Monroe in 1995.

16  Inga Arnyet: Atwater. Dames tells a dramatized version of this story in which there were several “KGB extrasensors.”

17  Swann not ready: The sources on Swann’s initial trainees are Swann, Dames, and two others from the program.

18  NSA backed out: Swann suggested to me that this was because Norfeld had done poorly in training. Others (one source from the research side, and two from the operations side) said that NSA had backed out for internal political reasons. McMoneagle specified that it was because the Norfeld-Henderson venture hadn’t received proper authorization.

19  secretive agency: Dames and Swann later implied that these trainees were from the special operations Delta Force, but a source in a better position to know denied this. McMoneagle said the Delta Force story was “a figment of somebody’s imagination.”

20  last four trainees: Swann, Dames, and another source familiar with the training program.

21  Kwajalein bilocation: A former member of the RV program.

22  Dames as “protégé”: Dames, in ordinary interviews and in Schnabel and Eagles 1995.

23  Swann’s true protégé; Swann, two other sources from the program.

24  Nance: Dames; Riley; Atwater; another source.

25  Nance’s sessions: When I first interviewed Swann at his home in late 1993,1 was able to see and take notes on many sessions Nance had done while in his advanced stages of training, including the May 1984 series.
